# Washington DC Unemployment Insurance — DOES

## Agency
DC Department of Employment Services (DOES)
- Website: does.dc.gov
- Online portal: dcnetworks.org (file and certify)
- Phone: 202-724-7000 (Mon–Fri 8:30am–4:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in DC during the base period
- Earned at least $1,300 in the highest-earning base period quarter
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $444/week
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 1/26th of your highest-earning base period quarter
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. File online at dcnetworks.org (24/7 availability; fastest)
2. File in person at a DOES American Job Center
3. Phone: 202-724-7000
4. File within the first week after job loss — there is a one-week waiting period
5. Certify every two weeks online at dcnetworks.org

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when DC receives a federal disaster declaration (less common as DC is a federal district, but presidential disaster declarations do apply)
- Covers workers and self-employed individuals not eligible for regular UI
- Apply through DOES within 30 days of DUA announcement

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 2 job contacts per week
- Record job search activities in the online system
- During federally declared disasters, DOES may temporarily suspend work search requirements
