# North Carolina Unemployment Assistance — Disaster Scenarios

## NC Division of Employment Security (DES) — Regular UI

### Benefit Amounts
- Weekly benefit: $15 to $350 (2024 maximum — one of the lowest caps in the US)
- Maximum duration: 12 weeks (reduced from 26 weeks during low unemployment periods)
- Base period earnings used to calculate weekly benefit amount

### Eligibility Requirements
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Must be able to work, available, and actively seeking employment
- Meet base period wage requirements
- Report to local NCWorks Career Center if requested

### How to Apply
1. Apply online at des.nc.gov
2. Phone: 888-737-0259 (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm ET)
3. Apply within 14 days of your last day of work to avoid backdating delays

### Key Note for NC Workers
NC's $350/week maximum is significantly below the national average. Workers should immediately pursue supplemental assistance (FEMA ONA, SBA loans, local nonprofits) alongside UI.

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA) in North Carolina
- Covers workers not eligible for regular UI (self-employed, 1099 contractors, gig workers)
- Must apply within 30 days of DUA announcement via des.nc.gov
- Benefits calculated using prior income (net profit for self-employed)

## North Carolina Common Disaster Areas
- Eastern NC — frequently impacted by hurricanes (Outer Banks, Bertie, Robeson counties)
- Western NC — flooding from hurricane remnants (Buncombe, McDowell, Rutherford)
- Statewide — significant exposure to Hurricane Helene-type events

## NCWorks Career Centers
Local career centers provide disaster recovery job services:
- Resume writing and job search assistance
- Skills assessments and training referrals
- Connection to Rapid Response services when businesses close

Find nearest office: NCWorks.gov/find-a-career-center

## NC On-the-Job Training and Retraining
- **NC Community College System**: Free and low-cost occupational training
- **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)**: Subsidized training for displaced workers
- **NC Works Customized Training**: Employer-specific training during disaster recovery
- Contact NCWorks Online at ncworks.gov for eligibility screening

## Supplemental Programs
- **NC Emergency Rental Assistance**: Available after major disaster declarations
- **NC Food Assistance**: Expedited SNAP (food stamps) available in declared counties
- **2-1-1 NC**: Call 211 for local emergency resources, rental assistance, utility help
