# Washington State Unemployment Insurance — ESD

## Agency
Washington State Employment Security Department (ESD)
- Website: esd.wa.gov
- Online portal: esd.wa.gov/unemployment (apply and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-800-318-6022 (Mon–Fri 8am–4pm PT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster evacuation, business closure)
- Worked in Washington during the base period
- Earned at least $3,000 in base period wages (across at least two quarters)
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: ~$1,019/week (one of the highest in the US; adjusted annually)
- **Benefit rate**: 60–70% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Washington's high benefit cap reflects the state's higher average wages — significant protection for displaced tech, aerospace, and trade workers

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at esd.wa.gov (24/7; strongly recommended)
2. Apply by phone: 1-800-318-6022
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every week online through esd.wa.gov

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Washington has significant wildfire, flood, and earthquake disaster history (Oso Landslide, Eastern WA wildfires, Cascadia subduction zone preparedness)
- Triggered when Washington receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through ESD within 30 days of DUA announcement date
- ESD posts disaster-specific resources at esd.wa.gov and pubs disaster notifications via media

## Washington-Specific: Wildfire and Flood Context
- Eastern Washington (Okanogan, Chelan, Ferry counties) frequently affected by wildfires
- Western Washington (King, Pierce, Snohomish) subject to flooding and earthquake risk
- DUA has been activated multiple times for wildfire-displaced workers — coverage for self-employed farmers and small business owners

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 verifiable job contacts per week
- Log contacts in the ESD online system
- Work search is routinely waived for wildfire and flood declared counties

## Key Resources
- ESD disaster page: esd.wa.gov (search "disaster")
- WorkSource Centers: Free job placement, career counseling, retraining; worksourcewa.com
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days of declaration)
