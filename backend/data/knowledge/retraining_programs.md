# Workforce Retraining Programs

## Federal Programs

### Trade Adjustment Assistance (TAA)
- For workers who lost jobs due to increased foreign imports or production shifts
- Provides: extended income support, job training funding, job search assistance, relocation allowances
- Apply through your state workforce agency
- Website: dol.gov/agencies/eta/tradeact

### Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)
- Federally funded, state-administered workforce development
- Free job training, resume help, career counseling
- Dislocated Worker Program specifically for laid-off workers
- Find your local American Job Center: careeronestop.org/LocalHelp

### Trade Readjustment Allowances (TRA)
- Extended UI benefits for workers enrolled in approved training while UI runs out
- Must enroll in training to qualify
- Contact your state UI office

## California Programs

### Employment Training Panel (ETP)
- Funds job training for employed and recently displaced workers
- Partners with community colleges and employers
- Free to workers; employer must apply
- Website: etp.ca.gov

### America's Job Center of California (AJCC)
- Free career services: resume, interview prep, job matching, skills training
- Located throughout California
- Dislocated worker services available immediately after job loss
- Find locations: ajcc.ca.gov

### California Community College System
- 116 community colleges offer workforce training, certificates, and degrees
- Low cost ($46/unit for CA residents), financial aid available
- High-demand programs: healthcare (CNA, medical coding), IT, construction technology, culinary

### High Road Training Partnerships (HRTP)
- California-funded sector-based training aligned with high-quality jobs
- Focus: healthcare, construction, tech, manufacturing
- Find programs: highroad.ca.gov

## Florida Programs

### CareerSource Florida
- Statewide workforce development system
- Free services at 100+ centers: job search, resume, training referrals
- Incumbent Worker Training and Quick Response Training programs
- Website: careersourceflorida.com

### Florida Ready to Work
- Free online certificate program covering workplace and technical skills
- Accepted by thousands of Florida employers
- Website: floridareadytowork.com

## Free Online Certifications

### Google Career Certificates
- Duration: 3-6 months
- Cost: ~$49/month on Coursera (financial aid available)
- Certificates: IT Support, Data Analytics, Project Management, UX Design, Cybersecurity, Digital Marketing
- Job placement: Google connects graduates to employer partners

### AWS Cloud Certifications
- Cloud practitioner to solutions architect tracks
- Free training materials at aws.amazon.com/training

### Coursera / edX
- Many free audit options
- Partner certificates from top universities and companies
- Financial aid available for low-income learners

### LinkedIn Learning
- Free with many public library cards
- Thousands of professional skill courses

## Disaster-Context Retraining Opportunities
After a major disaster, high-demand roles that can be reached with short training:
- **Construction / Skilled Trades**: Many community colleges offer 8-16 week certificates in electrical, plumbing basics, HVAC
- **Insurance Adjusting**: State licensing exams; preparation courses available in 4-8 weeks
- **Healthcare Support**: CNA certification typically 4-8 weeks; medical coding 3-6 months
- **CDL (Commercial Driver's License)**: 3-8 weeks; truck drivers in very high demand post-disaster for debris removal and supply logistics
- **Hazmat / Environmental Remediation**: OSHA 40-hour HAZWOPER certification; typically 1 week of training
