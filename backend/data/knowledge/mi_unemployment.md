# Michigan Unemployment Insurance — UIA

## Agency
Michigan Unemployment Insurance Agency (UIA)
- Website: michigan.gov/uia
- Online portal: MiWAM (Michigan Web Account Manager) at miwam.uia.state.mi.us
- Phone: 1-866-500-0017 (Mon–Fri 8am–5pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster closure)
- Worked in Michigan during the base period
- Earned at least $3,589 in at least two base period quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $362/week (among the lower caps in the Midwest)
- **Benefit rate**: 4.1% of your base period wages, up to the weekly cap
- **Duration**: Up to 20 weeks (Michigan has a shorter maximum than most states)
- Note: 20-week maximum — if your recovery takes longer, explore retraining programs early

## How to Apply
1. File online through MiWAM at miwam.uia.state.mi.us (24/7; required for most applicants)
2. File by phone: 1-866-500-0017
3. Apply within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks through MiWAM

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Activated when Michigan receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and others ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through UIA within 30 days of DUA announcement date
- UIA posts disaster-specific information at michigan.gov/uia

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 1 verified job contact per week
- Record contacts in MiWAM
- During disaster declarations, UIA may temporarily waive work search for affected counties

## Key Resources
- UIA disaster page: michigan.gov/uia
- Michigan Works! Centers: Free career services, retraining, job placement; michiganworks.org
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days of declaration)
