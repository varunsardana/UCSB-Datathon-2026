# Indiana Unemployment Insurance — DWD

## Agency
Indiana Department of Workforce Development (DWD)
- Website: in.gov/dwd
- Online portal: Uplink.in.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-800-891-6499 (Mon–Fri 8am–4:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster closure)
- Worked in Indiana during the base period
- Earned at least $2,500 in total during the base period
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $390/week
- **Benefit rate**: 47% of average weekly wage, up to the cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. File online at Uplink.in.gov (24/7; recommended)
2. File by phone: 1-800-891-6499
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period applies before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone through Uplink

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when Indiana receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed individuals and those who don't qualify for regular UI
- Apply through DWD within 30 days of DUA announcement
- DWD posts disaster-specific updates at in.gov/dwd

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 job contacts per week while collecting benefits
- Log contacts in the Uplink system — DWD audits records
- During federally declared disasters, DWD may waive work search requirements for affected counties
