# Nevada Unemployment Insurance — DETR

## Agency
Nevada Department of Employment, Training and Rehabilitation (DETR)
- Website: detr.nv.gov / ui.nv.gov
- Online portal: ui.nv.gov (file and certify online)
- Phone: 702-486-0350 (Las Vegas) / 775-684-0350 (Reno; Mon–Fri 8am–5pm PT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, business closure, disaster)
- Worked in Nevada during the base period
- Earned at least $400 in each of two or more base period quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $469/week
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Nevada's tourism and hospitality economy means construction and hospitality workers are significantly impacted by disaster declarations

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at ui.nv.gov (24/7; fastest method)
2. Apply by phone: 702-486-0350 (Las Vegas) or 775-684-0350 (Reno)
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online at ui.nv.gov

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when Nevada receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and others ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through DETR within 30 days of DUA announcement
- DETR posts disaster-specific updates at detr.nv.gov

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 1 verified job contact per week (Nevada has lighter requirement than most states)
- Document contacts in the online system
- Work search may be waived for counties under disaster declaration
