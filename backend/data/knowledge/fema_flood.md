# FEMA Flood Assistance Programs

## Overview
Floods are the most common natural disaster in the United States. FEMA provides multiple assistance programs for flood survivors.

## Individual Assistance (IA)
After a major flood disaster declaration, FEMA's Individual Assistance program offers:

- **Housing Assistance**: Up to $42,500 for temporary housing, home repairs, or replacement (2024 limits, adjusted annually)
- **Other Needs Assistance (ONA)**: Up to $42,500 for personal property losses, medical/dental expenses, transportation, and essential household items
- **Transitional Sheltering Assistance (TSA)**: Short-term hotel stays when temporary housing isn't available

### Eligibility Requirements
- Damage to primary residence in a declared disaster area
- U.S. citizen, non-citizen national, or qualified alien
- Cannot duplicate insurance benefits
- Apply within 60 days of disaster declaration

### How to Apply
1. Register at DisasterAssistance.gov or call 1-800-621-3362 (TTY: 1-800-462-7585)
2. Create a FEMA account and complete the application
3. FEMA inspector may visit your home to verify damage
4. Decision within 10 business days

## National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP)
- Separate from FEMA disaster grants; requires active policy
- Flood insurance pays regardless of disaster declaration
- Maximum coverage: $250,000 for structure, $100,000 for contents
- If you don't have flood insurance, FEMA grants are the primary resource

## SBA Disaster Loans for Flood Survivors
- Low-interest federal loans for homeowners, renters, businesses
- Homeowners: up to $500,000 for real estate
- Renters/homeowners: up to $100,000 for personal property
- Businesses: up to $2 million for physical damage or working capital
- Apply at SBA.gov/disaster or call 1-800-659-2955

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- If flood prevents you from working, apply for DUA within 30 days of announcement
- Covers self-employed, farmers, and gig workers not eligible for regular UI
- Benefits based on prior income, up to state UI maximum

## Key Deadlines
- FEMA IA registration: typically 60 days after declaration
- DUA applications: 30 days after DUA announcement
- SBA loan applications: typically 60 days for physical damage, 9 months for economic injury

## Industries Most Affected by Floods
- Construction (damage response surge, then slowdown)
- Retail and hospitality (customer base disrupted)
- Agriculture (crop and livestock losses)
- Transportation and logistics (road/port closures)
- Healthcare (facility damage, patient displacement)
