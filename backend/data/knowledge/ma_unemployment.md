# Massachusetts Unemployment Insurance — DUA

## Agency
Massachusetts Department of Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Website: mass.gov/dua
- Online portal: UI Online at mass.gov/dua (file and certify 24/7)
- Phone: 1-877-626-6800 (Mon–Fri 8:30am–4:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Massachusetts during the base period
- Earned at least $5,700 in your highest-earning base period quarter OR $3,800 total in all four quarters
- Able to work, available, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: ~$1,033/week (one of the highest in the US; adjusted annually)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 30 weeks (also higher than most states)
- Dependency allowances available: additional $25/week per dependent (up to $75/week extra)

## How to Apply
1. File online at mass.gov/dua (24/7; fastest and preferred)
2. File by phone: 1-877-626-6800
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify weekly online through UI Online

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Note: In Massachusetts, the unemployment agency is called DUA — different from federal DUA
- Federal Disaster Unemployment Assistance is triggered when Massachusetts receives a presidential disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed and agricultural workers not eligible for regular UI
- Apply through DUA within 30 days of federal DUA announcement date

## Massachusetts-Specific Programs
- **Extended Benefits (EB)**: Available during high unemployment periods; extends beyond 30 weeks
- **TRA (Trade Readjustment Allowances)**: For workers displaced due to trade; apply through DUA
- **Rapid Response**: If your employer is closing or doing mass layoffs, contact MassHire immediately (masshire.com)

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 contacts per week
- Log contacts in the UI Online system
- Massachusetts may waive work search for declared disaster areas
