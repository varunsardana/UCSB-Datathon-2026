# Louisiana Unemployment Assistance — Disaster Scenarios

## Louisiana Workforce Commission (LWC) — Regular UI

### Benefit Amounts
- Weekly benefit: up to $247/week (2024) — among the lowest in the US
- Maximum duration: 26 weeks under normal conditions
- Extended benefits may be available during high unemployment after major disasters

### Eligibility Requirements
- Lost job through no fault of your own (layoff, business closure, disaster-related reduction)
- Worked and earned wages in Louisiana in the past 12-18 months
- Able and available to work
- Actively seeking new employment

### How to Apply
1. Online: laworks.net/unemployment
2. Phone: 866-783-5567 (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm CT)
3. Apply within 14 days of job loss for earliest benefit start
4. Have SSN, work history, reason for separation ready

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA) in Louisiana
Louisiana has extensive DUA experience given frequent hurricane activity:
- Self-employed, farmers, and gig workers apply through LWC: laworks.net
- DUA applications must be submitted within 30 days of announcement
- LWC typically sets up special disaster assistance lines after major events

## Louisiana High-Risk Areas and Industries
**Most affected regions:**
- Southeast Louisiana (Orleans, Jefferson, St. Tammany) — hurricanes, flooding
- Coastal parishes (Terrebonne, Lafourche, Plaquemines) — storm surge
- Central Louisiana — tornados, severe storms

**Hardest-hit industries post-disaster:**
- Oil and gas (Gulf Coast operations disrupted by hurricanes)
- Tourism and hospitality (New Orleans dependent)
- Commercial fishing (coastal storm damage)
- Construction (initial surge, then normalization)
- Healthcare (hospital evacuations, facility damage)

## Louisiana Economic Development (LED) — Business Recovery
- Small Business Emergency Loan program post-disaster
- Connect with LED at opportunitylouisiana.com for business recovery resources

## Louisiana Fast Track Retraining
- **Louisiana Community and Technical College System (LCTCS)**: Low-cost certificates
- **LED FastStart**: Workforce training for businesses relocating/expanding after disaster
- **WIOA**: Federally funded training for displaced workers; apply through LWC

## Key Post-Disaster Benefits Available in Louisiana
1. FEMA Individuals and Households Program (IHP) — housing and personal property
2. Louisiana Restore Louisiana — homeowner assistance (when active)
3. Small Business Administration disaster loans
4. Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
5. Louisiana Gig Economy/1099 Worker Protection (through DUA)
