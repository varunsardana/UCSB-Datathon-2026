# Pennsylvania Unemployment Insurance — L&I

## Agency
Pennsylvania Department of Labor & Industry (L&I)
- Website: uc.pa.gov
- Online portal: pua.benefits.uc.pa.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-888-313-7284 (Mon–Fri 8am–4pm ET; call volume is high — use online where possible)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Pennsylvania during the base period
- Earned at least $116 per week on average during the base period
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $572/week (plus $8/week per dependent, up to additional $8/week × 3 dependents)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at uc.pa.gov (24/7; strongly recommended over phone)
2. Apply by phone: 1-888-313-7284 (expect long wait times during disasters)
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online through the UC system

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Pennsylvania has significant flood and severe storm disaster history (Tropical Storm Ida, Agnes)
- Triggered when Pennsylvania receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through L&I within 30 days of DUA announcement date
- L&I posts disaster-specific information at uc.pa.gov

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 2 job contacts per week
- Record contacts in the online UC system
- Work search may be waived for counties under a federal disaster declaration

## Key Resources
- PA L&I disaster page: uc.pa.gov and dli.pa.gov (search "disaster")
- PA CareerLink Centers: Free job placement, career counseling, retraining; careerlink.pa.gov
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days of declaration)
