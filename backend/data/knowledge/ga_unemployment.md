# Georgia Unemployment Insurance — DOL

## Agency
Georgia Department of Labor (GDOL)
- Website: dol.georgia.gov
- Online portal: dol.georgia.gov/file-claim (file and certify online)
- Phone: 1-877-709-8185 (Mon–Fri 8am–4:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster-related closure)
- Worked in Georgia during the base period
- Earned at least $1,134 in at least two quarters of the base period
- Able, available, and actively looking for work

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $365/week
- **Minimum weekly benefit**: $55/week
- **Duration**: 12–20 weeks (Georgia adjusts duration based on state unemployment rate; lower than many states)
- Note: Georgia has a shorter maximum duration than most states — plan your budget accordingly

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at dol.georgia.gov (available 24/7; preferred method)
2. Phone: 1-877-709-8185
3. Apply within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every week online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Activated when Georgia receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, farm workers, and others not covered by regular UI
- Apply through GDOL within 30 days of the DUA announcement
- GDOL sets up dedicated disaster hotlines — check dol.georgia.gov after a disaster declaration

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 1 verifiable job contact per week
- Georgia may temporarily suspend work search requirements for counties under a disaster declaration

## Key Disaster Resources
- GDOL disaster page: dol.georgia.gov (search "disaster")
- Georgia WorkSource Centers: Free job placement and retraining; worksourcega.com
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days)
