# Colorado Unemployment Insurance — CDLE

## Agency
Colorado Department of Labor and Employment (CDLE)
- Website: coloradoui.gov
- Phone: 303-318-9000 (Denver metro) / 1-800-388-5515 (outside Denver; Mon–Fri 8am–4pm MST)
- MyUI+ portal: myui.coworkforce.com (file and certify online)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from work through no fault of your own
- Worked in Colorado during the base period
- Earned at least $2,500 in the base period
- Able and available to work

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: ~$800/week (adjusted annually; check coloradoui.gov for current cap)
- **Benefit rate**: 55% of your average weekly wage, up to the cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at myui.coworkforce.com (available 24/7; fastest method)
2. Apply by phone: 303-318-9000 or 1-800-388-5515
3. File within the first week after job separation
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Activated when Colorado receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers workers and self-employed individuals who don't qualify for regular UI
- Apply through CDLE within 30 days of the DUA announcement date
- CDLE announces DUA via coworkforce.com and media releases

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make 5 job contacts per week while collecting UI
- Log contacts in MyUI+ system — CDLE audits records
- During disaster declarations, CDLE may temporarily waive work search for affected counties

## Key Disaster-Specific Resources
- Colorado CDLE disaster info: coworkforce.com
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days of declaration)
- Colorado Workforce Centers: Free job search and retraining; find locations at coworkforce.com
