# Florida Reemployment Assistance — DEO

## Basic Eligibility
- Lost job through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster-related business closure)
- Earned at least $3,400 in wages in your base period
- Able and available to work and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Weekly benefit**: 1/26th of your highest quarter wages
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $275 per week
- **Duration**: 12 to 23 weeks depending on the state's unemployment rate
- Note: Florida's maximum benefit ($275/week) is among the lowest in the nation

## How to File
1. File online at connect.myflorida.com/CONNECT (fastest)
2. Phone: 1-800-204-2418
3. Apply within 14 days of your last day of work
4. **One-week waiting period** before payments begin
5. Must request benefit payments every two weeks online

## Disaster Reemployment Assistance (DRA)
Florida activates Disaster Reemployment Assistance (equivalent to federal DUA) after federal disaster declarations:
- Covers self-employed, gig workers, and those who don't qualify for regular RA
- Apply at connect.myflorida.com within 30 days of the DRA announcement
- Benefits match regular Reemployment Assistance amounts and duration

## Hurricane-Related Provisions
After major hurricanes (Florida's most common major disaster):
- DEO may waive the one-week waiting period
- Work search requirements may be suspended in affected counties
- CareerSource Florida centers in affected areas often set up mobile units for immediate assistance
- Call 1-800-342-3450 for disaster-specific guidance

## Florida Workforce Resources
- CareerSource Florida: Free job placement, resume help, skills training. Find centers at careersourceflorida.com
- FloridaJobs.org: Job listings and career services
- Employ Florida: employflorda.com — statewide job board
- Quick Response Training (QRT): Employer-driven training programs for new or expanding businesses
