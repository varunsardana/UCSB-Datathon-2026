# Illinois Unemployment Insurance — IDES

## Agency
Illinois Department of Employment Security (IDES)
- Website: ides.illinois.gov
- Online portal: ides.illinois.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-800-244-5631 (Mon–Fri 8:30am–5pm CT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from work through no fault of your own
- Worked in Illinois during the base period (first four of last five completed quarters)
- Earned at least $1,600 in the base period with earnings in at least two quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $742/week (plus $25/week per dependent child, up to additional $145.50)
- **Benefit rate**: 47% of your average weekly wage during the two highest-paid base period quarters
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. File online at ides.illinois.gov (24/7; fastest method)
2. File by phone: 1-800-244-5631
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week unpaid waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Activated when Illinois receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers and those who don't qualify for regular UI
- Apply through IDES within 30 days of the DUA announcement date
- IDES posts disaster-specific information at ides.illinois.gov after declarations

## Illinois WARN Act
- Illinois has its own WARN law: applies to employers with 75+ employees
- Requires 60-day advance notice for layoffs of 25+ employees (lower threshold than federal WARN)
- Contact IDES or the Illinois Department of Commerce if you suspect a WARN violation

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 job contacts per week
- Record contacts in the IDES online system
- Work search may be waived during a disaster for affected counties — check announcements
