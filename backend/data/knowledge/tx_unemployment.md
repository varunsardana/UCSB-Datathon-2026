# Texas Unemployment Assistance — Disaster Scenarios

## Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) — Regular UI
Texas administers regular unemployment insurance through the Texas Workforce Commission.

### Benefit Amounts
- Weekly benefit: $68 to $521 (2024)
- Maximum duration: 26 weeks
- Determined by earnings in the base period (first 4 of last 5 completed calendar quarters)

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must have earned at least $2,500 in the base period
- Must be unemployed through no fault of your own
- Must be able to work, available to work, and actively looking for work
- Must file initial claim within 14 days of job separation for disaster-related layoffs

### How to Apply
1. Apply online at Unemployment.Texas.gov
2. Call TWC Tele-Center: 800-939-6631 (Mon-Fri 8am-6pm CST)
3. Provide Social Security number, work history, employer name/address, separation reason

### After Filing
- TWC sends payment within 2-3 weeks if eligible
- File payment request every 2 weeks at Unemployment.Texas.gov
- Report any earnings during the benefit week

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA) in Texas
For workers who don't qualify for regular UI but lose jobs due to a declared disaster:
- Applies to self-employed, farmers, independent contractors, gig workers
- Must apply within 30 days of DUA announcement
- Apply through TWC using same portal: Unemployment.Texas.gov

## Texas Back to Work Initiative
After a disaster, TWC partners with employers to fast-track hiring:
- WorkInTexas.com job board updated with disaster-area openings
- Job fairs coordinated with Texas Workforce Solutions offices
- On-the-job training (OJT) subsidies for employers in affected areas

## Major Disaster-Affected Texas Counties (Reference)
- Harris County (Houston) — FIPS 48201 — frequently flooded
- Harris + Fort Bend + Montgomery regularly affected by Gulf hurricanes
- West Texas counties: tornadoes and severe storms
