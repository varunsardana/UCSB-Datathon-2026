# Rhode Island Unemployment Insurance — DLT

## Agency
Rhode Island Department of Labor and Training (DLT)
- Website: dlt.ri.gov
- Online portal: UIOnline at dlt.ri.gov (file and certify online)
- Phone: 401-415-6772 (Mon–Fri 8:30am–3:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Rhode Island during the base period
- Earned at least $3,000 in total base period wages or earned wages in 20 weeks of the base period
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: ~$898/week (among the highest in New England; adjusted annually)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 57.5% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks, with possible extended benefits

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at dlt.ri.gov (24/7; fastest method)
2. Apply by phone: 401-415-6772
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every week online through UIOnline

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when Rhode Island receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through DLT within 30 days of DUA announcement
- DLT posts disaster-specific updates at dlt.ri.gov

## Rhode Island-Specific
- **TDI (Temporary Disability Insurance)**: Rhode Island is one of few states with TDI — if you're unable to work due to injury in a disaster, this provides short-term income (separate from UI)
- Apply for TDI at dlt.ri.gov/individuals/worker-benefits/tdi-tci

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 job contacts per week
- Log contacts in the online system
- Work search may be waived for declared disaster areas
