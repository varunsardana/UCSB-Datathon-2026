# Oklahoma Unemployment Insurance — OESC

## Agency
Oklahoma Employment Security Commission (OESC)
- Website: oesc.state.ok.us
- Online portal: oesc.state.ok.us (file and certify online)
- Phone: 405-525-1500 (Oklahoma City area) / 1-800-555-1554 (statewide; Mon–Fri 8am–4:30pm CT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Oklahoma during the base period
- Earned at least $1,500 in one quarter of the base period
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $539/week
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Oklahoma has significant tornado and severe storm disaster history — DUA is commonly activated

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at oesc.state.ok.us (24/7; fastest method)
2. Apply by phone: 405-525-1500 or 1-800-555-1554
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Oklahoma is one of the most frequently disaster-declared states (tornadoes, floods, severe ice storms)
- DUA is triggered when Oklahoma receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through OESC within 30 days of DUA announcement
- OESC typically sets up tornado/storm recovery hotlines — check oesc.state.ok.us

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 1 verifiable job contact per week
- Record contacts in the online system
- Work search is routinely waived for counties under tornado or severe storm disaster declarations

## Key Resources
