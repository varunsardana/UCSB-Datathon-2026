# FEMA Wildfire Assistance Programs

## FEMA Individual Assistance (IA)
FEMA's Individual Assistance program provides financial help and services to disaster survivors after a wildfire declaration. Eligible applicants may receive:
- **Rental Assistance**: Funds for temporary housing while your home is being repaired or rebuilt
- **Home Repair/Replacement**: Grants to repair or replace your damaged home
- **Personal Property Replacement**: Funds for essential household items
- **Medical and Dental Expenses**: Covers disaster-caused medical costs
- **Moving and Storage**: Helps with temporary relocation costs

### How to Apply
- Online: DisasterAssistance.gov
- Phone: 1-800-621-3362 (TTY: 1-800-462-7585)
- FEMA Mobile App
- Apply within 60 days of the disaster declaration date

### Eligibility Requirements
- You must live in a county that received a federal disaster declaration
- Your home must have been damaged or destroyed by the disaster
- Your losses must not be covered by insurance (or insurance must be insufficient)

## FEMA Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
If you lost your job because of a wildfire and are not eligible for regular state unemployment:
- DUA provides weekly benefits to workers displaced by presidentially declared disasters

## SBA Disaster Loans (Wildfire)
The U.S. Small Business Administration offers low-interest disaster loans:
- **Businesses**: Up to $2 million for physical and economic injury
- **Homeowners**: Up to $500,000 for real estate damage
- **Renters**: Up to $100,000 for personal property
- Interest rates as low as 4% for businesses, 1.563% for homeowners
- Apply at: SBA.gov/disaster or call 1-800-659-2955

## Wildfire-Specific Labor Market Impact
After major wildfires, the following labor market patterns are common:
- Hospitality and tourism sectors (hotels, restaurants) see 20-40% employment drops in affected areas
- Entertainment and retail often experience temporary closures (10-30% employment decline)
- Construction and debris removal demand surges 100-300% within the first 3 months
- Healthcare workers (ER, mental health) see increased demand in weeks 1-4
- Insurance adjusters and claims processors see large demand spikes (months 1-6)
- Environmental remediation workers are needed for months 3-18

## Crisis Counseling
FEMA funds Crisis Counseling Programs (CCP) after disaster declarations:
- Free mental health services for disaster survivors
- Outreach workers help connect displaced workers with employment resources
- Contact your county mental health department or 211 to find local services
