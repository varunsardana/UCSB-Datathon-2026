# FEMA Hurricane Assistance Programs

## FEMA Individual Assistance (IA) — Hurricane
After a presidential major disaster declaration for a hurricane, eligible survivors can receive:
- **Temporary Housing**: Direct housing or rental assistance for displaced residents
- **Home Repair Grants**: Up to $43,900 for essential repairs (amount adjusts annually)
- **Personal Property**: Replacement of essential household items lost in flooding
- **Transportation Assistance**: Help replacing or repairing disaster-damaged vehicles
- **Unemployment Assistance**: If you lost work due to the hurricane (see DUA below)

### How to Apply
- DisasterAssistance.gov (fastest)
- 1-800-621-3362
- Apply within 60 days of disaster declaration

## Hurricane Labor Market Patterns
Post-hurricane workforce disruption follows a predictable curve:
- **Weeks 1-2**: Emergency evacuation and response. Healthcare (ER, triage), public safety, utilities surge.
- **Month 1**: Hospitality, retail, restaurants closed or severely reduced (30-60% employment loss in flood zones)
- **Months 1-3**: Construction, roofing, debris removal demand spikes dramatically (+200-400%)
- **Months 3-12**: Insurance claims adjusters in very high demand. Mental health workers needed.
- **Months 6-24**: Long-term rebuilding. Skilled trades (electricians, plumbers, HVAC) remain in demand.

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA) — Hurricane
- Weekly benefits if you lost your job directly due to the hurricane
- Also covers self-employed individuals whose businesses are impacted
- Apply through your state workforce agency within 30 days of DUA announcement
- Duration typically matches the state's disaster period (often 13-26 weeks)

## SBA Economic Injury Disaster Loans
For businesses that experienced economic losses:
- Up to $2 million for small businesses, nonprofits
- Covers operating costs you can't meet due to disaster
- 30-year loan terms, rates around 4%
- Apply: SBA.gov/disaster

## Housing Resources Post-Hurricane
- **HUD Housing**: HUD may open emergency voucher programs in affected areas
- **Red Cross**: Short-term shelter and emergency financial assistance
- **State Housing Finance Agencies**: Low-interest loans for homeowners in affected counties
