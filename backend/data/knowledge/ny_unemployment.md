# New York Unemployment Insurance — DOL

## Agency
New York State Department of Labor (NYSDOL)
- Website: dol.ny.gov
- Online portal: labor.ny.gov/signin (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-888-209-8124 (Mon–Fri 8am–5pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in New York during the base period (first four of last five completed quarters)
- Earned at least $2,900 in the base period with earnings in at least two quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $504/week
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at labor.ny.gov (24/7; preferred and fastest)
2. Apply by phone: 1-888-209-8124
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when New York receives a presidential major disaster declaration (NY has significant hurricane and severe storm history — Hurricane Sandy, Ida)
- Covers self-employed workers, farm workers, and others ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through NYSDOL within 30 days of DUA announcement date
- NYSDOL sets up dedicated disaster relief lines — check dol.ny.gov after a declaration

## New York WARN Act
- New York has a stricter WARN law than federal: 90-day notice required (vs 60 days federally)
- Applies to employers with 50 or more full-time employees
- Layoffs of 25+ employees (if 33% of workforce) trigger WARN
- Contact NYSDOL or an employment attorney if you suspect a violation

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 verifiable job contacts per week
- Record contacts in the online NY.gov system
- During federally declared disasters, NYSDOL may waive work search for affected counties
