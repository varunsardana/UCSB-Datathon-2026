# Ohio Unemployment Insurance — ODJFS

## Agency
Ohio Department of Job and Family Services (ODJFS)
- Website: unemployment.ohio.gov
- Online portal: unemployment.ohio.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-877-644-6562 (Mon–Fri 8am–5pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from work through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster-related closure)
- Worked in Ohio during the base period
- Earned at least $2,500 in total base period wages
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $647/week (plus $1–$52/week per dependent, up to $103/week)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 50% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Ohio offers dependency allowances — one of the few states with this provision

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at unemployment.ohio.gov (24/7; preferred method)
2. Apply by phone: 1-877-644-6562
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Triggered when Ohio receives a presidential major disaster declaration (Ohio has significant flood and severe storm history)
- Covers self-employed workers and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through ODJFS within 30 days of DUA announcement
- ODJFS posts disaster-specific information at jfs.ohio.gov and unemployment.ohio.gov

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 2 verifiable job contacts per week
- Log contacts in the online system
- Work search may be waived for counties under a federal disaster declaration

## Key Resources
- ODJFS disaster page: jfs.ohio.gov (search "disaster")
- OhioMeansJobs Centers: Free career services, retraining, resume help; ohiomeansjobs.com
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days)
