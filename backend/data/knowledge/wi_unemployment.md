# Wisconsin Unemployment Insurance — DWD

## Agency
Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development (DWD)
- Website: dwd.wisconsin.gov/ui
- Online portal: my.unemployment.wisconsin.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 608-232-0678 (Madison area) / 414-435-7069 (Milwaukee area; Mon–Fri 7:30am–4:30pm CT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Wisconsin during the base period (first four of last five completed quarters)
- Earned at least $2,050 in at least two base period quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $370/week (plus $4/week per dependent, up to $58/week additional)
- **Benefit rate**: 4% of your total base period wages, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Dependency allowances available for qualifying dependents

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at my.unemployment.wisconsin.gov (24/7; preferred)
2. Apply by phone: 608-232-0678 or 414-435-7069
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every week online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Wisconsin has significant flooding history (Mississippi and Fox River flood zones, heavy rainfall events)
- Triggered when Wisconsin receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through DWD within 30 days of DUA announcement
- DWD posts disaster-specific updates at dwd.wisconsin.gov

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 4 verifiable job contacts per week (Wisconsin has a higher requirement)
- Log contacts in the online system
- Work search may be waived for counties under a federal disaster declaration

## Key Resources
- DWD disaster page: dwd.wisconsin.gov (search "disaster")
- Wisconsin Job Centers: Free career services, retraining, job placement; jobcenterofwisconsin.com
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days)
