# Tennessee Unemployment Insurance — DLWD

## Agency
Tennessee Department of Labor and Workforce Development (DLWD)
- Website: tn.gov/workforce
- Online portal: jobs4tn.gov (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-844-224-5818 (Mon–Fri 8am–4pm CT)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own (layoff, disaster-related closure)
- Worked in Tennessee during the base period
- Earned at least $780.01 in at least two base period quarters
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $275/week (one of the lowest caps in the US)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 26 times the highest-earning base period week, divided by 26
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks
- Important: $275/week is significantly below average US cost of living — apply for all supplemental programs simultaneously

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at jobs4tn.gov (24/7; preferred method)
2. Apply by phone: 1-844-224-5818
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every week online or by phone

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Tennessee has significant severe storm, flood, and tornado disaster history
- Triggered when Tennessee receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through DLWD within 30 days of DUA announcement
- DLWD posts disaster updates at tn.gov/workforce

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 3 verifiable job contacts per week
- Log contacts in jobs4tn.gov
- Work search may be waived for counties under a federal disaster declaration

## Given the Low Benefit Cap — Additional Programs to Apply For Immediately
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov — apply same day as UI claim
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster — for workers with business income
- WIOA Dislocated Worker Program: Free retraining; local Tennessee American Job Center (see jobs4tn.gov)
- Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Economic hardship may qualify you

## Key Resources
- DLWD disaster page: tn.gov/workforce (search "disaster")
- Tennessee American Job Centers: Free job placement and retraining; jobs4tn.gov
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
