# Virginia Unemployment Insurance — VEC

## Agency
Virginia Employment Commission (VEC)
- Website: vec.virginia.gov
- Online portal: vec.virginia.gov/individuals (file and certify online 24/7)
- Phone: 1-800-897-5630 (Mon–Fri 8:15am–4:30pm ET)

## Basic Eligibility
- Separated from employment through no fault of your own
- Worked in Virginia during the base period (first four of last five completed quarters)
- Earned at least $3,000 in total base period wages
- Able to work, available for work, and actively seeking employment

## Benefit Amounts
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: $378/week (below average nationally)
- **Benefit rate**: Approximately 54% of your average weekly wage, up to cap
- **Duration**: Up to 26 weeks

## How to Apply
1. Apply online at vec.virginia.gov (24/7; recommended)
2. Apply by phone: 1-800-897-5630
3. File within the first week after job loss
4. One-week waiting period before first payment
5. Certify every two weeks online through the VEC portal

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
- Virginia has hurricane and severe flooding history (Hurricane Floyd, Tropical Storm Ida, Nor'easters)
- Triggered when Virginia receives a presidential major disaster declaration
- Covers self-employed workers, agricultural workers, and those ineligible for regular UI
- Apply through VEC within 30 days of DUA announcement
- VEC posts disaster-specific updates at vec.virginia.gov

## Work Search Requirements
- Must make at least 2 verifiable job contacts per week
- Log contacts in the VEC online portal
- Work search may be waived for counties under a federal disaster declaration

## Key Resources
- VEC disaster page: vec.virginia.gov (search "disaster")
- Virginia Employment Centers: Free career services, job placement, retraining; vec.virginia.gov/locations
- SBA Disaster Loans: sba.gov/disaster
- FEMA Individual Assistance: disasterassistance.gov (apply within 60 days)
